3. Courses and Credits

This section centralizes the rules on the student’s curriculum, including the validation of credits from courses and publications.

Study Plan

PG-EAM does not have a set of mandatory courses. The study program is flexible and must be defined by each student in conjunction with their advisor.

  • Course Selection: Courses should be chosen to support the research project.
  • Validation: The study plan is approved by the area representative to ensure that the chosen courses are aligned with the focus of the dissertation or thesis.
  • Course Catalog: The list of offered courses and their syllabi is available in the ITA Graduate Programs Catalog, published annually.

Source: PG-EAM Internal Regulations, Section 4.2.

Counting Credits from Publications

PG-EAM awards credits for scientific articles published while the student was enrolled as a regular student. The student must be the first author, and the advisor must be a co-author.

Journal Articles

The score is based on the highest percentile of the journal in the year of publication, according to the Scopus or Journal Citation Reports (JIF) databases.

Tier Criterion (Percentile) Credits
P1 percentile ≥ 95 3.0
P2 90 ≤ percentile < 95 3.0
P3 85 ≤ percentile < 90 2.5
P4 75 ≤ percentile < 85 2.5
P5 62.5 ≤ percentile < 75 2.0
P6 50 ≤ percentile < 62.5 1.5

Attention: The Qualis CAPES system has been discontinued by the Engineering III area and is no longer used for credit attribution.

Source: PG-EAM Internal Regulations, Section 7.1.

Conference Papers

Credits are awarded for full papers, peer-reviewed and presented at the respective event. The classification follows the CAPES criteria for the Engineering III area.

Conference Classification Credits
International 1.00
Supranational 0.75
National 0.50
Local/Regional 0.25

Requirement for Doctorate: To meet the minimum requirement of 3 publication credits for the Doctorate (according to ICA 37-356), only conferences of International and Supranational character will be counted.

Source: PG-EAM Internal Regulations, Sections 7.2 and 7.3.

Transferring Credits from Other Institutions

It is possible to transfer credits from graduate-level courses taken at other institutions, provided they have not been used for the completion of previous degrees.

  • Requirement: The course syllabus must be compatible with the topic of the dissertation or thesis.
  • Equivalence: Every 48 class hours correspond to 3 credits at PG-EAM.
  • Process: The request must be made by the student to the area representative, and final approval is given by the program’s council (CPPG-EAM).

Source: PG-EAM Internal Regulations, Section 8.
