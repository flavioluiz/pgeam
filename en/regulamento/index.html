PG-EAM Regulations

This section details the most important rules and procedures for Master’s and PhD students of the Graduate Program in Aeronautical and Mechanical Engineering (PG-EAM).

The information is organized to guide students throughout their graduate studies, focusing on the main events and processes. The complete regulations are described in ICA 37-356 and the PG-EAM Internal Regulations.

Browse the subsections to find the information you are looking for:

  • 1. New Student’s Guide: Essential information about the selection process, enrollment, and scholarship criteria.

  • 2. Your Journey in the Program: Details on academic life milestones, such as transitioning to a regular student, deadlines, and extensions.

  • 3. Courses and Credits: Rules on the study plan, counting credits from publications, and transferring credits from external courses.

  • 4. Final Exams: Qualification and Defense: Guidelines on the PhD qualification process and the dissertation/thesis defense.

  • 5. Special Procedures: Information on the direct PhD path, changing advisors, withdrawal, and readmission to the program.

This section gathers the essential information for those starting in the Graduate Program in Aeronautical and Mechanical Engineering (PG-EAM).

To enter PG-EAM, the candidate must be approved in the selection process, which involves document analysis and exams. For details on how to apply, please visit the Admission Process page.

Application Requirements:

  • Fulfill all documentary and procedural application requirements with the Dean of Graduate Studies Office (IP), according to ICA 37-356.

2. Your Journey in the Program

This section details the milestones and processes that define a student’s academic life at PG-EAM.

From Special Student to Regular Student

Students who enter as “special” (in a preparatory program) can request to transition to “regular” status to continue their course, with more details and a form to be filled out by the advisor: http://www.ita.br/posgrad/migracaodeespecialpararegular.

Application Process:

  1. The student formalizes the request to their advisor through a detailed technical report.
  2. The advisor evaluates the report and provides an opinion, forwarding the request to the area representative.
  3. The area representative analyzes the request and, if favorable, communicates the decision to the program’s council (CPPG-EAM) and the IP’s secretariat for implementation.

3. Courses and Credits

This section centralizes the rules on the student’s curriculum, including the validation of credits from courses and publications.

Study Plan

PG-EAM does not have a set of mandatory courses. The study program is flexible and must be defined by each student in conjunction with their advisor.

  • Course Selection: Courses should be chosen to support the research project.
  • Validation: The study plan is approved by the area representative to ensure that the chosen courses are aligned with the focus of the dissertation or thesis.
  • Course Catalog: The list of offered courses and their syllabi is available in the ITA Graduate Programs Catalog, published annually.

Source: PG-EAM Internal Regulations, Section 4.2.

Process:

5. Special Procedures

This section covers less common but important situations, such as changing levels, changing advisors, and the processes of withdrawal and readmission to the program.

Transfer to Direct Doctorate

Master’s students with exceptional performance may request to transfer to the Direct Doctorate program, without needing to complete a dissertation.

  • Process: The transfer must be approved by the program’s council (CPPG), based on the student’s academic performance, the advisor’s productivity, and the quality of the thesis proposal.
  • Requirements: For the transfer, the student must present an English proficiency certificate (according to IP criteria) and meet the additional criteria defined in the program’s Internal Regulations.
  • Deadlines: The maximum deadlines for completing the Direct Doctorate are counted from the student’s first enrollment date in the Master’s program.

Source: ICA 37-356, Section 3.5.
